# Road Trip Strategy — Delfa AI 2-Week Sales Blitz

## Executive Summary

**Recommended route:** Week 1 Texas, Week 2 Florida.
**Why:** These two states contain 94 of our 254 FPO hot lead targets (37%) and $165M of total target revenue (64%). More importantly, they house the headquarters of our two highest-value network deals: DM Clinical (20 sites, $79M) and Alcanza (23 sites, $45M, competitor customer).

**Core strategy:** Network-first selling. Instead of visiting 94 individual sites, we focus on closing 2-3 network HQ deals that unlock 40+ sites each, supplemented by meetings with high-scoring independent sites along the route.

---

## Prioritization Framework

We score every site on a hierarchy — each layer filters and ranks:

| Factor | Weight | Rationale |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Hot lead status | Gate (required) | CRIO list was curated for product-fit. Only hot leads are worth visiting. |
| Competitor customer | +50 pts | Already pays for recruitment tools. Conversation is "why switch" not "why buy." |
| Revenue $5M+ | +40 pts | High trial volume = acute recruitment pain = strong Delfa value prop. |
| Has contact info | +30 pts | Can pre-book meetings. Cold walk-ins have <10% success rate. |
| FPO status | +20 pts | Independent decision-makers. No procurement committees. |
| High-volume therapeutic area | +10 pts | Infectious Disease, Oncology, Cardiology, Endocrinology need the most patients. |
| Existing customer | -200 pts | Already closed. Use as reference accounts, don't re-sell. |

**Key insight:** Revenue alone doesn't tell the story. A $2.4M Alcanza HQ site (score: 205) outranks a $33.9M AMR Knoxville site (score: 170) because Alcanza is a competitor customer with 23 sites — the deal multiplier matters more than single-site revenue.

## Week 1: Texas (53 targets, $118M)

### Why Texas

1. **DM Clinical Research HQ** — 20-site network, $79M combined revenue, headquartered in Houston. One enterprise deal = 20 sites onboarded. Their Infectious Disease focus means high-volume trials with constant recruitment pressure — exactly where Delfa's value prop is strongest.

2. **Independent site density** — Even removing DM Clinical, Texas has 35+ independent hot lead FPO sites representing $39M. Pillar Clinical ($15.5M, Psychiatry), ACRC Trials (4 TX locations), Juno Research ($3.7M), SMS Clinical ($3.7M).

3. **Texas Triangle geography** — Houston, Dallas, San Antonio, and Austin form a 300-mile triangle. All four metros have hot lead clusters, making route efficiency natural without over-optimizing for geography.

## Week 2: Florida (41 targets, $47M)

### Why Florida

1. **Alcanza Clinical Research HQ (Lake Mary)** — 23-site network, $45M combined revenue, **Alleviate competitor customer**. This is the single warmest lead in our entire dataset. They already pay for a competing product, understand the value, and may be evaluating alternatives.

2. **Site density in central Florida** — Lake Mary/Orlando alone has 17 hot lead targets. Tampa/Ocala adds 21 more. These two metros are 90 minutes apart — high meeting density.

3. **Therapeutic diversity** — FL targets span Infectious Disease, Endocrinology, Neurology, Rheumatology. This tests Delfa's cross-specialty appeal and generates diverse case studies.

## Pre-Trip Playbook

1. **2 weeks before:** Email top 20 targets via Clay Outreach contacts. Subject: "Visiting [City] — 30 min for Delfa demo?" Use existing customer names as social proof.
2. **1 week before:** LinkedIn connect with site directors and PIs. Comment on their recent publications or trial announcements.
3. **Day before each metro:** Confirm meetings. Have backup targets in case of cancellations.
4. **During meetings:** Demo Delfa. Ask about current recruitment workflow, pain points, and budget cycle. For competitor customers: "What does Alleviate do well? Where does it fall short?"
5. **Same day follow-up:** Thank-you email with customized ROI calculation based on their trial volume.
