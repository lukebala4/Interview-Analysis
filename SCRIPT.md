# Delfa AI GTM Road Trip — Talk Track & Script

*Use this to walk through the strategy verbally. Each section is a talking point.*

## Opening

> "We were given two datasets — 4,551 US clinical trial sites and 482 curated hot leads — and asked: if you had 2 weeks to drive across the country and close as many deals as possible for Delfa's patient recruitment platform, where would you go and why?"

---

## How We Approached It

> "We didn't start with a map. We started with a question: **who actually buys this product?**"

### Step 1: Filtered to the right buyers

- Delfa sells to **For-Profit Organizations (FPOs)** — independent research sites that need help finding patients for clinical trials
- We excluded NPOs (MD Anderson, Memorial Sloan Kettering, etc.) — they have internal recruitment teams, long procurement cycles, and aren't the target
- We excluded 17 existing Delfa customers — already closed, but valuable as reference accounts
- **Result:** narrowed 4,551 sites down to 254 actionable FPO hot lead targets

### Step 2: Scored every target

> "Not all hot leads are equal. A competitor customer with $10M in trial volume and a LinkedIn contact for the site director is worth 10x more than a $200K site with no website."

We built a scoring framework:

| What we scored | Why it matters |
|---------------|---------------|
| **Is it a hot lead?** (+100 pts) | The curated list was vetted for product-fit. These sites match Delfa's value prop. |
| **Competitor customer?** (+50 pts) | Alleviate/Grove customers already budget for recruitment tools. The conversation is "why switch" not "why buy." |
| **Revenue $5M+?** (+40 pts) | More revenue = more trials = more patients needed = more value from Delfa. |
| **Have contact info?** (+30 pts) | If we can email or LinkedIn the decision-maker before arriving, our meeting rate jumps from <10% to 40%+. |
| **FPO status?** (+20 pts) | Independent sites decide fast. No procurement committee. |

### Step 3: Discovered the network insight

> "This is where it got interesting. When we looked at the parent companies behind these hot leads, we found that many of them are **multi-site networks**. DM Clinical Research isn't 1 site — it's 20 sites across Texas. Alcanza isn't 1 site — it's 23 sites across Florida."

> "That changes the entire math. Instead of visiting 94 individual sites, we can close 2-3 network headquarters and unlock 40+ sites from 2-3 meetings."

| Network | Sites | Payments | What it means |
|---------|-------|----------|--------------|
| DM Clinical Research | 20 | $79M | Visit Houston HQ → access 20 sites |
| Alcanza Clinical | 23 | $45M | Visit Lake Mary HQ → access 23 sites. They're an Alleviate competitor customer. |
| AMR | 16 | $43M | Knoxville anchor. $34M single site — highest in our dataset. |

## The Plan

> "Week 1: Texas. Week 2: Florida. Here's why."

### Why Texas first?

- **55 FPO hot leads** — most of any state by a wide margin
- **DM Clinical HQ is in Houston** — one meeting potentially opens 20 sites worth $79M
- The Texas Triangle (Houston → Dallas → San Antonio → Austin) is a natural driving loop, ~300 miles between stops
- Independent sites like Pillar Clinical ($15.5M, Psychiatry) and Pinnacle ($2.8M, 8-site network) fill the rest of the week

- **Alcanza is the single warmest lead in the entire dataset** — they're already paying Alleviate (a competitor) for a similar product. They understand the value prop. The conversation skips "why do I need this" and goes straight to "why is Delfa better?"
- 23-site network means one deal has massive multiplier value
- 41 additional FL hot leads fill the week (Renstar/Phasewell $8.7M, Complete Health $7M)
- **$47M total payment volume** across 41 targets

## Assumptions We Made

These are the bets embedded in the strategy. Each one is worth discussing.

### 1. "Revenue = recruitment need"

> **Assumption:** Sites with higher sponsor payments run more trials, need more patients, and therefore get more value from Delfa.

> **Why it could be wrong:** A $50M site might already recruit brilliantly. A struggling $500K site might desperately need help but can't afford it.

> **Our position:** Revenue is the best proxy we have for trial volume. We don't have data on enrollment timelines or recruitment success rates. The sweet spot is $1M–$15M — big enough to have budget, small enough to lack internal recruitment teams.

### 2. "Closing means opening relationships"

> **Assumption:** We're not signing contracts in 2 weeks. Enterprise clinical trial software has a 3–6 month sales cycle. The trip fills the pipeline.

> **Our position:** The goal is to book first meetings, deliver demos, and build relationships. Pre-trip outreach via the 203 enriched contacts (email, LinkedIn) is what makes this work. "I'll be in Houston next week visiting DM Clinical — would love 30 minutes" creates urgency and social proof.

### 3. "Network HQs make centralized decisions"

> **Assumption:** DM Clinical's Houston HQ decides for all 20 sites. Alcanza's Lake Mary HQ decides for all 23 sites.

> **Why it could be wrong:** Some networks let individual sites choose their own tools. If so, "1 deal = 20 sites" becomes "1 deal = 1 site."

> **Our position:** Even if individual sites decide independently, Texas still has 35 non-DM hot leads. The network HQ meeting is a bonus multiplier, not the only reason to go to Texas.

### 4. "Competitor customers are warmest"

> **Assumption:** Sites already paying Alleviate or Grove for recruitment tools will be most receptive to Delfa.

> **Why it could be wrong:** They might be locked into multi-year contracts. They might be happy with what they have.

> **Our position:** Even if locked in, meeting them now plants the seed for renewal season. They already understand the product category — we don't have to sell the concept, just the switch. And we get competitive intelligence on what Alleviate does well and where it falls short.

### 5. "FPOs over NPOs, always"

> **Assumption:** We completely excluded NPOs from the road trip.

> **Why it could be wrong:** Some NPOs (university hospitals with smaller programs) might actually need recruitment help and have simpler procurement.

> **Our position:** For a 2-week blitz, we need fast decisions. FPOs are independent businesses with a single decision-maker. NPOs have committees, IRB approvals, and vendor onboarding processes. The math doesn't work in 14 days.

### 6. "Geography matters less than deal quality"

> **Assumption:** We chose TX + FL over higher-revenue-per-site regions like California ($50M across 22 sites).

> **Why California loses:** The sites are spread across LA, Sacramento, San Diego, and San Francisco — 400 miles apart. You'd spend more time on the freeway than in meetings. Texas and Florida have tighter geographic clusters.

> **Trade-off:** We're leaving $143M on the table (CA, MA, TN, NC). That becomes Month 2.

## Answering the Hard Questions

### "DM Clinical is 1 deal, not 20. Your Texas numbers are inflated."

> "Fair point. If DM Clinical is centrally managed, visiting their 8 Texas locations is redundant — we visit HQ once. But even removing DM Clinical entirely, Texas still has 35 independent FPO hot leads worth $40M. Combined with the DM HQ visit as upside, Texas remains the #1 cluster."

### "Why not California? Higher revenue per site."

> "Revenue per site is higher, but meeting density is lower. Hope Clinical is in Canoga Park, Benchmark Research is in Sacramento, Science 37 is in San Francisco. That's 3 meetings across 400 miles and 3 days of driving. In Texas, we can hit 4 meetings in Houston alone. We'll do California as a focused 3-day trip in Month 2."

### "You can't close enterprise deals in 2 weeks."

> "Correct. We're not trying to. The 2-week trip is about filling the pipeline — first meetings, demos, and building relationships. The actual close happens over the following 3-6 months. But the in-person meeting is what gets us in the door. These are busy clinicians who don't take cold calls. Showing up with a demo and a referral from an existing Delfa customer is the highest-conversion channel."

### "What if Alcanza is locked into Alleviate?"

> "Three scenarios. Best case: they're unhappy and evaluating alternatives — we show up at the right time. Base case: they're satisfied but curious — we get competitive intelligence and a warm relationship for when the contract renews. Worst case: they decline the meeting — we still have 41 other FL hot leads to fill Week 2. The trip is worth it regardless."

### "What about AMR Knoxville? $34M is huge."

> "It's the highest single-site payment volume in our entire hot lead dataset. But it's one meeting in Knoxville, Tennessee — off the TX/FL route. One meeting doesn't fill a week. We've flagged it as the #1 target for the Month 2 trip (Boston → NYC → Wilmington NC → Knoxville TN)."

### "Your scoring weights are arbitrary."

> "They are. We don't have historical conversion data to calibrate them. But the hierarchy is defensible: hot lead status is the gate (these were curated for product-fit), competitor customers are warmer than cold leads (they already budget for this), and revenue correlates with trial volume. The specific point values matter less than the rank order they produce. And we sanity-checked: the top 5 targets (DM Clinical, Alcanza, AMR, Hope, Pillar) pass the gut check."

### "Revenue isn't the same as recruitment need."

> "Agreed. Revenue tells us trial volume, not recruitment difficulty. A site running 50 trials a year has more recruitment events than a site running 5 — but the small site might struggle more per trial. We don't have enrollment timeline data. Revenue is an imperfect proxy, but it's the best signal in this dataset."
